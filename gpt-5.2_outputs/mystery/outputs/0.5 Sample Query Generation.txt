% Query 1: Attack is executable when harmony holds and the target is both a province and a planet; after attack, target is in pain and harmony is false. (satisfiable)
:- query
    0: harmony & isProvince(a) & isPlanet(a) & ~inPain(a);
    0: attack(a);
    maxstep: inPain(a) & ~isProvince(a) & ~isPlanet(a) & ~harmony.

% Query 2: Attack is not executable if harmony does not hold (even if the target is a province and a planet). (unsatisfiable)
:- query
    0: ~harmony & isProvince(a) & isPlanet(a) & ~inPain(a);
    0: attack(a).

% Query 3: Succumb is executable only when the entity is in pain; after succumb, pain is removed and harmony becomes true and the entity becomes both province and planet. (satisfiable)
:- query
    0: ~harmony & inPain(b) & ~isProvince(b) & ~isPlanet(b);
    0: succumb(b);
    maxstep: ~inPain(b) & isProvince(b) & isPlanet(b) & harmony.

% Query 4: Overcome is not executable if the "other" entity is not a province (even if the acting entity is in pain). (unsatisfiable)
:- query
    0: inPain(a) & ~isProvince(b);
    0: overcome(a,b).

% Query 5: Feast is executable only if the acting entity craves the other, the acting entity is a province, and harmony holds; after feast, acting entity is in pain, loses province, loses the craving, harmony becomes false, and the other becomes a province. (satisfiable)
:- query
    0: harmony & isProvince(a) & craves(a,c) & ~inPain(a) & ~isProvince(c);
    0: feast(a,c);
    maxstep: inPain(a) & ~isProvince(a) & ~craves(a,c) & isProvince(c) & ~harmony.
