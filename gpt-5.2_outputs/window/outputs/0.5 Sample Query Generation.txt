% Query 1: Open w1 when it is closed and unlocked. This should be allowed and result in w1 being open. (satisfiable)
:- query
    0: isClosed(w1) & isUnlocked(w1) & ~isOpen(w1) & ~isLocked(w1);
    0: openWin(w1);
    maxstep: isOpen(w1) & ~isClosed(w1).

% Query 2: Try to open w1 while it is locked (and closed). Opening requires unlocked, so this should be rejected. (unsatisfiable)
:- query
    0: isClosed(w1) & isLocked(w1) & ~isUnlocked(w1) & ~isOpen(w1);
    0: openWin(w1).

% Query 3: Lock w1 when it is closed and unlocked. This should be allowed and result in w1 being locked. (satisfiable)
:- query
    0: isClosed(w1) & isUnlocked(w1) & ~isLocked(w1) & ~isOpen(w1);
    0: lockWin(w1);
    maxstep: isLocked(w1) & ~isUnlocked(w1).

% Query 4: Try to lock w2 while it is open and unlocked. Locking requires closed, so this should be rejected. (unsatisfiable)
:- query
    0: isOpen(w2) & isUnlocked(w2) & ~isClosed(w2) & ~isLocked(w2);
    0: lockWin(w2).

% Query 5: Achieve the main goal with an explicit 2-step plan: open w1, then lock w2 (closing w2 first if needed). (satisfiable)
:- query
    0: isClosed(w1) & isUnlocked(w1) & ~isOpen(w1) & ~isLocked(w1) &
       isOpen(w2) & isUnlocked(w2) & ~isClosed(w2) & ~isLocked(w2);
    0: openWin(w1);
    1: closeWin(w2);
    2: lockWin(w2);
    maxstep: isOpen(w1) & isLocked(w2).
