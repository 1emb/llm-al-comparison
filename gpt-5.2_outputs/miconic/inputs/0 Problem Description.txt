This domain models an elevator moving among floors to pick up and deliver passengers. Floors are identified as floors, passengers are identified as passengers, each passenger has an origin floor where they can be picked up and a destination floor where they want to be dropped off, and an ordering relation states when one floor is above another; the elevator is always at exactly one floor as indicated by its current location. A passenger can board the elevator when the relevant location is a floor, the person is a passenger, the elevator is currently at that floor, and that floor is the passenger’s origin; boarding makes the passenger count as having boarded. A passenger can depart the elevator when the relevant location is a floor, the person is a passenger, the elevator is currently at that floor, that floor is the passenger’s destination, and the passenger has already boarded; departing removes the fact that the passenger is boarded and instead marks the passenger as served. The elevator can move upward from one floor to another when both locations are floors, the elevator is currently at the starting floor, and the starting floor is above the destination floor; after moving, the elevator is at the destination floor and no longer at the starting floor. The elevator can move downward from one floor to another when both locations are floors, the elevator is currently at the starting floor, and the destination floor is above the starting floor; after moving, the elevator is at the destination floor and no longer at the starting floor.
