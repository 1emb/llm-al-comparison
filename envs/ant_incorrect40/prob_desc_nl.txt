An ant can move from one location to another only if it is currently at the starting location, the two locations are adjacent, and the destination location is clear; after moving, the ant is no longer at the starting location and is instead at the destination. An ant can pick up food at its current location only if it is at that location, food is present there, and the ant is not already carrying food; after picking up, the ant becomes a carrier of food and the food is no longer present at that location. An ant can drop food at a nest location only if it is at that location, that location is a nest, and the ant is carrying food; after dropping, the ant is no longer carrying food and food becomes present at the nest location. Finally, an ant can clear an obstacle in a neighboring location only if it is at some location, the blocked location is adjacent to it, and that neighboring location is not clear; after clearing, the previously blocked location becomes clear.
